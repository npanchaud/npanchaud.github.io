About me

What started as a geographer's journey, then took a turn towards cartography and urban planning. I finally figured out I liked drawing and coloring plans and maps. My feet then took me into the wonderful and ever-changing world of web cartography and mapping. I also like to wander on the open data and open source trails. During my doctoral studies, I was conducting research in cartographic functionality for non-expert users on geoportals. It involved creating smart functions that help creating more beautiful maps on the geoportal as well as opening cartographic knowledge. Furthermore, I also taught graduate courses in web mapping that included theory, hands-on exercises, and semester projects.
Now I am providing Web-GIS solutions to our clients in the area of Transportation and Infrastructure Asset Management at Hexagon Schweiz AG.
I am passionate about open (geo)data, openstreetmap and beautiful maps (old and new, on paper and online).

Portfolio

GEOIDEA.RO The Geoportal

The GEOIDEA.RO project was the base of my doctoral research. It aimed at improving the scientific basis for open geodata model adoption in Romania. This project is co-financed by a grant from Switzerland through the Swiss Contribution to the enlarged European Union via the Swiss-Romanian Cooperation Programme.

  • GEodata Open Initiative for Development and Economic Advancement in Romania
  • 2013 - 2015
  • More Info: website
City Street Network

A friend of mine really liked the îlots urbains and asked for something similar. I ended up with a bi-colored street network map. Metropoles work really well for this kind of design. Each city has thus a unique "streetprint".

  • OpenStreetMap data
  • November 2016
Fridge Magnets

The Swiss Society of Cartography organized a christmas market stand selling only map-related products. With some colleagues we made use of old prints of the Swiss World Atlas to create unique fridge magnets.

  • Thanks to Aline and Mirjam for the fun craft afternoons
  • November 2016
Indian Convicts

As part of a seminar entitled "People on the Move: Migration, Assimilation and Diasporas in Modern History (1750 - 2000)", I realized the following map on the flux of convicts sent to penal colonies during the 18th-19th centuries in the British Indian Empire.

  • October 2013
  • Map: full pdf
Îlots urbains

Using OpenStreetMap data (roads and coastlines), I turned city plans into modern and colorful images for decoration purposes. I followed the very-well done tutorial from Maxime Résibois

  • Affoltern Am Albis, Switzerland: linien canvas
  • Lausanne, Switzerland: custom smartphone cover
  • Toyko, Japan: A0 poster
OSGEoLab

I am part of the OSGeoLab at ETHZ and some colleagues and I prepared an interactive map to show the partner labs around the world on our project website. We used Leaflet and data from the official website.

  • 2013-2017
GEOIDEA.RO The Geoportal Lite

With the GEOIDEA.RO project, we are providing all the data from the geoportal as WMS as well. This Geoportal Lite exemplify the use of our web services with third party libraries, here Leaflet. The code repository for this project is public.

  • Webpage: link
GeoHackathon Dec 5, 2015

Based on the GEOIDEA.RO Geoportal Lite, I built a small web map illustrating archeological sites and museums along the Danube (within a 3km buffer) that can be subjected to flooding. This activity took palce during a hackathon and on an original idea from Nicolaie Constantinescu.
